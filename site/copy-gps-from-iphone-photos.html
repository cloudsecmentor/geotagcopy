Phone GPS reference workflow

Copy GPS from iPhone photos to camera photos

If your iPhone captured location but your DSLR or mirrorless camera did not, GeoTagCopy can use the iPhone photos as GPS donors for nearby camera photos from the same trip or shoot.

When this works well

This workflow is strongest when you took occasional iPhone photos in the same places where you used your camera. GeoTagCopy reads timestamps, finds nearby untagged photos, and lets you review the matches before it writes metadata.

Why not use a GPX file?

GPX tracklogs are useful, but they require planning ahead. GeoTagCopy is aimed at the common after-the-fact problem: your phone photos already have GPS, your camera photos do not, and you want a local desktop tool that can bridge the two.

Basic steps

  1. Select iPhone photos. Pick the folder that contains geotagged reference images.
  2. Select camera photos. Pick the folder with untagged JPG, HEIC, or RAW-derived files.
  3. Review matches. Check the timestamp and location groups before applying tags.
  4. Write GPS locally. GeoTagCopy uses ExifTool to write the coordinates.
